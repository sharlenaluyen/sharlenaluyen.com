Philanthropy

For over 7 years, I served as a Court Appointed Special Advocate in Benton and Multnomah Counties. Court Appointed Special Advocates (CASA) are specially trained community volunteers appointed by Juvenile and Domestic Relations Court Judges to speak in the best interest of children who are brought before the court for reasons of abuse or neglect. We are an official part of the judicial proceedings, working alongside attorneys, social workers and other professionals.

Wanting more from the judicial system, I also served as a board member on the Citizen's Review Board (CRB) in Multnomah County for 2 years to bring transparency and public oversight to foster care cases. Specifically, attorneys, social workers, and other judicial members were expected to justify decisions made throughout the case. The board would submit their recommendations on future proceedings for judges to review. We were able to shape public policy affecting Oregon’s most vulnerable children and families.

Snorkeling with Baby Humpback Whale in Tahiti

During my work hiatus from 2023-2024, I traveled to Tahiti to snorkel with wild humpback whales. There are a lot of rules put in place to protect these guys; we are with guides the entire time, unable to approach, staying at least 100 meters away, wearing biodegradable sunscreen, etc. However, as you can see, they are incredibly playful and are curious about us. This baby humpback whale jumped no more than 10 feet in front of me while its mama was deeper down below. I've had the privilege to snorkel with humpback whales, whale sharks, turtles, sting/manta rays, manatees, and so many more in the wild!
