Why 1Password

I'm not just applying.
I'm already a believer.

I have been a 1Password user for years. Not because someone told me to, but because I understand what's at stake when credentials are managed poorly. Working inside enterprise identity systems at HashiCorp taught me exactly how much damage a single leaked secret can do. 1Password is the product I actually trust with mine. I remember the days when we just Slack'ed passwords around, and I don't miss it.

🔐
I use the product
I don't need to be sold on the value of password management. I'm already a user and someone who genuinely advocates for it. When people ask me what app they should download right now, I say 1Password before they finish the question.
🏠
Remote is my natural habitat
I have been fully remote since my first job in tech. I work best when I can focus deeply without interruption; no open office noise, no one tapping my shoulder, and yes, the freedom to take a restroom break without timing it around a meeting room schedule. Async culture makes me a better engineer. I'm well-versed in remote communication norms and tools, and I know how to build relationships across the organization without watercooler chats. 1Password's remote-first approach is a huge draw for me.
📐
Cryptography was my favorite class
I had a math minor and the course that stuck with me most was cryptography. The elegance of the problem space, the number of ways to approach it, and the real-world stakes made it feel like the most honest kind of math. 1Password lives in that space every day.
🏢
Enterprise identity is what I know
At HashiCorp, I built and maintained the SCIM and SAML API layer: the integration surface enterprise customers actually touch when they connect their identity providers. I know what it feels like to own the part of the system where credentials cross the boundary. That's exactly the territory 1Password operates in.
